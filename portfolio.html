UI Design Principles

  1. Clear navigation: The navigation bar stays fixed at the top of the page no matter where you scroll or click, so you can always return to where you started or jump to a new section. Project pages also include a button at the bottom to return to the project menu, so visitors never hit a dead end.
  2. Consistent Branding: I built a unified visual identity by setting custom CSS properties for color and design patterns across the entire site. To help visitors get to know me beyond my resume, I wove in the three things I love most—engineering, ballroom dance, and the ocean—into the site's tone and color palette from top to bottom.
  3. Visual Hierarchy & Readability: The layout follows an F-pattern to guide the eye naturally through the page. Text size and contrast are tuned for easy reading, and information is grouped into clear visual "blocks" so visitors always know what to look at next. Spacing is deliberate, tight enough to avoid feeling sparse, open enough to avoid feeling crowded.
  4. Self-Explanatory Interface: Every interactive element (buttons, nav links, cards) is labeled with text, an icon, or both, so visitors can navigate the entire site intuitively without needing outside instructions.
  5. Responsive Design: The site adapts to different screen sizes from desktop to mobile, so the experience holds up no matter what device someone's using.
  6. Scannable Information: Technical skills and project details are organized into tables and tag-style groupings rather than dense paragraphs, so recruiters and engineers can quickly scan for relevant qualifications without digging through blocks of text.

UX Research & Iteration

Goal: To optimize the site for clarity, consistency, and user agency (the ability for users to control their experience).

Methodology: Conducting a qualitative usability study where participants review the live site and provide feedback via a structured Google Form and/or semi-structured interviews. This data-driven approach identifies friction points and informs future design iterations.
